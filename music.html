10 Ways that You Can Make Music A Political Act

1. Consider the role of music in society: Rhythm and harmony are the metronome of subjectivity. (Plato)

2. Challenge the rules, as musical notation is political. (Jean-Jacques Rousseau) We can change that (e.g., by making it easier for others to "read" music).

3. Democratize the stage: create the conditions for fans and audiences to become part of the song. (Apichatpong Weerasethakul does this with cinema in the 2000 film Mysterious Object at Noon)

4. Create a music wing within an existing social movement.

5.Sing more! Or make it possible for others to sing more. John Street writes that a song of resistance is political "in the mere act of singing." (p. 44)

6.Explore music about your region as a way to sustain local/indigenous cultures.

7. Utilize music as "a form of reporting" (See Street 2011, p. 48)

8. The personal is political. Document your political awakening in music. Which songs or musical events (1,2) would you share?

9. DIY: Grab the nearest musical instrument and make your message either as loud or clear as possible. Keep in mind, many bands were terrible musicans (e.g., The Ramones, and/or just about every other punk band of the late 70s). But the whole, says Aristotle, is greater than th sum of its parts.

10. Learn from the mistakes of others.
