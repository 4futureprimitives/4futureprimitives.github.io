Sovereign   Writers   Subjects   Sound  






Rhythm and harmony are the metronome of subjectivity. (Plato) To read sound is not just to read musical notation. (See Jean-Jacques Rousseau) Reading sound is reading landscapes and the amplitudes of space. So we might sing more or louder, or make it possible for others to sing more. In this sense, John Street writes that a song of resistance is political "in the mere act of singing." (p. 44)

But singing into the digital ocean does not ensure any kind of recognition or reception. Sometimes what is needed is noise. Filmmakers like Puhipau and Joan Lander of Nā maka o ka ʻāina amplified the voices of Hawaiʻi in the cries of eviction and political struggle. So it is not just music, but the resonance of sound itself that functions as "a form of reporting" (See Street 2011, p. 48)
