Sovereign Subjects

Sovereign   Writers   Subjects   Sound  

The Thai scholar Thongchai Winichakul stated that reading literacy is the way out of a political deadlock. In Western theory, knowledge builds power in the rethinking of how language (or discourse) works to encapsulate, even compose, subjects. The observation ranges from Michel Foucault's use of the term genealogy to Julia Kristeva and Roland Barthes' emphasis on breaking the conventions of language by re-reading the world of voices (see "geno-text") or, for instance, in the decomposition of music (see "geno-song"). A subject is a body and a thematic composition rooted in the language of the past (the archive) and the genres of the political (the composition of voices).
