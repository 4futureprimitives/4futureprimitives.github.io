In Thailand, students and youth culture are calling for an overhaul of the post-Coup system established in the military restructuring of power since September 19th 2006. Central to that system is the military-monarchy nexus and the dissolution of the 1997 "People's constitution, and (most recently) an appointed senate since the 2016 Constitutional Referendum. As a democratic alternative, protestors are asking for the resignation of a military general, the orchestration of a more participatory constitution (galvanized by a participatory petition format called Ilaw), and the reform of the Thai monarchy.
