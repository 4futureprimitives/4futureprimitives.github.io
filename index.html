In Thailand, students and youth culture are calling for the reform of its post-Coup system, which has strenthened a military-monarchy nexus since the dissolution of the "People's constitution" in 2006, and an appointed senate in 2016. As a democratic alternative, they are asking for the resignation of a military general, the orchestration of a more participatory constitution (galvanized by a petition systemm called Ilaw), and the reform of the Thai monarchy.
